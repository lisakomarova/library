1. The Eve of All Saints - Ray Bradbury

For those who are serious enough to read children's books, but still dream of a drop of magic in their lives, the book of the master of mysteries Ray Bradbury is perfect. In the center of events is the company of boys, who, together with Mr. Smerch, must save her friend on the very eve of All Saints Day. The colorful and lively history creates an atmosphere of mystery and Great Mystery, so it will be an excellent choice for a cold October evening. It is worth noting that the book is not only about ordinary European witches, but also the priests of Ancient Egypt and the Celtic Druids - everyone who possessed unusual skills.

2. Moscow Watchdogs - Larisa Romanovskaya

In every district of our capital, at least one Watchtower should work - a witch who monitors the balance of energy and negative events in the zone entrusted to her. Watchdogs are not immortal, go through several cycles of rebirth and recovery, and are forced to lose their loved ones time after time. And though the feeling that someone is watching you makes your day a little more capable, the witches themselves have a very difficult time.

"Getting truth in your eyes is even worse than gasoline."

5. Marina - Carlos Ruiz Safon

Gothic history is devoted to the meaning of death in the lives of different people: for some it causes unlimited melancholy, for someone it has a passionate desire for immortality. The plot is based on two related stories: Dr. Mikhail Kalvenik and his wife, as well as the young Oscar and Marina, fascinated by secrets and riddles stored in an abandoned house.

"Thought is the most precious thing in the world, and the thought bearer is the brain, so protect your head from cold."

6. Halloween Party - Agatha Christie

The novels of Agatha Christie are again in vogue - in the near future we will see another adaptation of the adventures of Hercule Poirot. If you are just now discovering the creativity of the chief master of detectives - start with this autumn book, imbued with the spirit of mysterious events. During the eve of All Saints, a little girl dies, telling frightening stories about someone else's murders, but this event does not seem to affect the inhabitants of a small prim town. The visiting writer is not ready to leave the case without attention and calls Poirot for help.

"From what you hear and read these days, it seems that murder, under certain circumstances, becomes acceptable to a large part of society."
