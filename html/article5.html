5 books that will allow you to develop a creativity

Creativity is very important for personal development. Only lazy does not write about this, but what if the muse does not come, there is no inspiration, and there are no ideas? New book review How to Green is dedicated to all creative people and those who want to develop their creative vein. With the books listed in the review, you will be more consciously aware of your work and you will not lack creative ideas.

1. “I want more ideas” - Steve Rowling

About the author: Steve Rowling - producer on development, teacher of the program of creative management approach at the Air Force Academy. Creative thinking consultant.

Why read: The book contains over a hundred techniques and exercises for the development of creative thinking. The book “I want more ideas” can be viewed as a two-in-one guide: quick solutions and detailed explanations.

What this book is about in two words: According to Steve Rowling, to create more creative ideas, you need to use divergent thinking. Divergent thinking is a search for solutions beyond the obvious. For example, instead of looking for one correct solution, you offer a huge number of options. Emma Coates, an artist of Pixar, gives this advice: “Drop the first idea that you had. And also the second, third, fourth, fifth - drop all the obvious. Surprise yourself. ” The book “I want more ideas” will stimulate your creative spirit. And if, on the contrary, you have a fountain of ideas, it will help to take them under control.

2. "In Search of Ideas" - Grant Snyder

About the author: Orthodontist by profession, Grant Snyder since 2009 has been drawing daily philosophical strips (comics) at night: during the day he goes to his usual, non-creative work. Do you still think you don't have time for creativity? Strips Snyder draws for his newsletters and magazines like The New York Times.

Why read: “Looking for ideas” is an illustrated study of creativity. We are intrigued!

What this book is about in two words: It shows the process of creativity from different sides: it is multitasking, and improvisation, and the workflow, and the inner world, and the study of oneself. After reading the comics on the creative theme (there are plenty of them in the book), you will definitely get inspired and go to create. If you want to know how ideas look, where they come from, whether you can touch them and even how to catch the thought by the tail, then you will like the book. Great motivator! The author himself says that the book will be especially useful for those who are faced with procrastination or a blank sheet.

3. “Quest for a creative person” - Stefan Bucher

About the author: Stefan Bucher is a writer, graphic designer and illustrator.

Why read: “Quest for a creative person” - there are as many as 344 questions about how to find inspiration, not to be derailed and become a pro.

What this book is about in a nutshell: The author believes that the secret of creative success lies in asking the right questions. Responding to them, you will understand your life, understand what to strive for, and learn how to achieve what you want. Creativity in the book is considered as an ordinary sphere of life. To achieve a result, talent and luck alone are not enough: you need to work a lot. The book offered a lot of questions from famous writers, artists, musicians, photographers and Oscar laureates. Celebrities share the questions they were asked at the beginning of their career. Everyone agrees that it helped them a lot in their careers and creative work. Bonus - cool illustrations that the author himself drew. And further. Stefan Bucher assures that even if you answer only half of the questions, your life will change. For the better, of course.

4. “Calligraphy brushes. Tips, techniques and ideas for creativity "- Anna Suvorova

About the author: Anna Suvorova is a popular instagram artist, creator of the online school of calligraphy and lettering.

Why read: This is not just a book, but a practical book. The author promises that the book can replace the basic course of calligraphy.

What this book is about in a nutshell: By engaging in calligraphy, you develop the right hemisphere - it’s responsible for creativity. In addition, improve your reaction and orientation in space. Henry Kao, a Chinese calligraphy researcher, says that we are experiencing a special kind of concentration in the process. Yes, and it is good for health. The breathing is leveled, the pressure decreases, muscular tension decreases. We hope we have convinced you how useful this type of art therapy is. Thanks to the book you will master the art of calligraphy for a month from scratch. The book contains many exercises to train basic skills and creativity, and you can train right in the book - for this there are special recipes. Through the barcodes, instructional videos are available.

5. “Where Good Ideas Come From” - Stephen Johnson

About the author: Stephen Johnson is an American writer, a specialist in new media, the author of non-fiction bestsellers (“Everything bad is good for you”, “The invention of air”).

Why read: The title of the book speaks for itself. There are a lot of cool ideas around us. But where do they come from. A really important topic for those who want to develop in themselves a creative beginning and understand the mechanisms of action of this difficult phenomenon. As a bonus, you will learn how to work and create with maximum efficiency. In addition to theory, you are waiting for practical tools for inspiration. The result is not far off!
